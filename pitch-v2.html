↟ 02 Why nothing stopped it

↟ 02 Why nothing stopped it

Every existing layer was built for a human at a modal — or protects a slice, not the agent.

The universal gap

  • Wallets protect an account, not an agent. An agent operates across many wallets, tools, and rails.
  • Blowfish, Blockaid warn a human. There is no human at the modal.
  • Spending caps assume risk scales with amount. A zero-dollar authority change is catastrophic.
  • Generic risk scoring asks "does this look malicious?" — not "was this authorized?"

Each named competitor · why it misses this attack

  • AmberTrace — formal-verified policies. A $150 transfer that meets the rules still passes; the mandate violation is invisible.
  • Kredit — enterprise ML risk scoring. A zero-dollar authority change scores as zero risk.
  • ATBASH — client-defined red lines. Only catches what the client thought to add.
  • Google AP2 — signs Intent + Cart + Payment mandates. A compromised agent can still produce a valid signed mandate.
  • AWS Bedrock AgentCore — Cedar policy pre-signing. AWS-only, deterministic; no reasoning on ambiguous cases.

↟ 04 What Compass does

↟ 04 What Compass does

Every transaction, verified against mandate, before it settles.

  • Non-bypassable by construction

    We sit on the signature, not on the interface. Co-signer via Squads, not SDK. A compromised agent cannot skip us — the money does not move without our verdict.

  • Intent, not amount

    The question is "was this authorized for this agent?", not "does this look bad?" A clean transfer to a never-authorized address is exactly the attack.
